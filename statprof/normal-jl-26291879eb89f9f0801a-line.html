1 """
2 Normal(μ,σ)
3
4 The *Normal distribution* with mean `μ` and standard deviation `σ≥0` has probability density function
5
6 ```math
7 f(x; \\mu, \\sigma) = \\frac{1}{\\sqrt{2 \\pi \\sigma^2}}
8 \\exp \\left( - \\frac{(x - \\mu)^2}{2 \\sigma^2} \\right)
9 ```
10
11 Note that if `σ == 0`, then the distribution is a point mass concentrated at `μ`.
12 Though not technically a continuous distribution, it is allowed so as to account for cases where `σ` may have underflowed,
13 and the functions are defined by taking the pointwise limit as ``σ → 0``.
14
15 ```julia
16 Normal() # standard Normal distribution with zero mean and unit variance
17 Normal(mu) # Normal distribution with mean mu and unit variance
18 Normal(mu, sig) # Normal distribution with mean mu and variance sig^2
19
20 params(d) # Get the parameters, i.e. (mu, sig)
21 mean(d) # Get the mean, i.e. mu
22 std(d) # Get the standard deviation, i.e. sig
23 ```
